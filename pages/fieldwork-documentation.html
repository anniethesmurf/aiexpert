Fieldwork & Documentation

Fieldwork grounds the entire heritage system in real environments, materials, communities, and expert practice.

Documentation is designed not only for archival preservation, but for future modelling, learning deployment, and research analysis.

Documentation Layers

Video and observation

Recording process, gesture, timing, and interaction in practice.

Process notes

Structuring steps, conditions, materials, and decision points.

Interviews and elicitation

Surfacing intent, evaluation criteria, and reflective expert commentary.

Metadata and indexing

Making documentation reusable across AI models, research, and education systems.

From Site to System

Fieldwork produces the raw material for heritage knowledge systems: evidence, process traces, contextual meaning, and validated records that can later support digital twin construction.

Well-structured fieldwork turns fragile practice into durable foundations for modelling, learning, and collaboration.
