AI+Expert Core

AI+Expert Core System

Modeling, scaling, and transmitting human expertise through AI-driven systems

We transform tacit human knowledge into structured, learnable, and scalable AI systems.

Core statement

We build AI that learns how experts think, enabling the scalable transfer of tacit knowledge.

Tacit knowledge embedded in expert practice remains difficult to capture, formalise, and scale. Traditional master–apprentice models are increasingly constrained, while existing AI systems primarily focus on content delivery rather than modelling expert cognition and decision-making. This limits their effectiveness in practice-based and skill-intensive domains.

This project develops the AI Expert Twin framework, a novel approach that models expert knowledge as structured, computable representations of procedural actions and decision processes. Situated within Artificial Intelligence in Education, the project shifts from content-centric AI towards cognition-centric modelling, enabling AI systems to generate adaptive, expert-informed guidance for learners.

The research is conducted through a collaboration between The University of Sydney and the Beijing Institute of Fashion Technology, using a cultural heritage workshop as a living laboratory for data collection, modelling, and validation.

By transforming expert knowledge into scalable AI systems, the project contributes to advances in AI-driven education, supports cultural knowledge preservation, and establishes a transferable framework applicable to domains such as vocational training and creative industries.

Research Aims

  1. Develop the AI Expert Twin framework as a structured model for capturing, representing, and scaling tacit expert knowledge.
  2. Design and implement AI-based systems that translate structured expert representations into adaptive, expert-informed learner guidance.
  3. Evaluate the effectiveness of the framework and systems in supporting scalable learning, knowledge preservation, and expert-informed practice.

Research Questions

  • How can tacit expert knowledge be systematically captured and represented in a computable form?
  • How can structured representations of expert cognition and decision-making be transformed into adaptive AI guidance for learners?
  • To what extent can AI models built on expert-informed representations support scalable learning and knowledge transfer in practice-based domains?

Scope

The project focuses on modelling expert knowledge within controlled real-world environments where tacit cognition, procedural action, and situated decision-making can be observed, captured, and validated. The immediate application is a cultural heritage workshop context, while the framework is designed to support broader transfer into other practice-based domains.

Core Innovation

Core Innovation & Conceptual Framework

This project introduces the AI Expert Twin as a new model for transforming tacit expert knowledge into structured, AI-enabled systems that can support learning, interpretation, and guided practice.

Conceptual Advancement

Positioned within Artificial Intelligence in Education, the project shifts the focus of AI from content delivery toward modelling expert cognition, judgment, and procedural reasoning.

Methodological Innovation

The project integrates multimodal data capture, structured modelling, and iterative validation to build computable representations of expert thinking from real-world practice.

Application Innovation

By embedding the framework within a real-world cultural heritage workshop, the research demonstrates how expert-informed AI systems can support knowledge preservation, scalable education, and transferable domain applications.

This project advances AI in education by modelling expert cognition and decision-making as computable systems, enabling the scalable representation and application of tacit knowledge beyond content-based approaches.
